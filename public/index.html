Biography

I am a 3rd year Ph.D. student in the Department of Biostatistics, Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health, advised by Rajarshi Mukherjee. My research interests are primarily in the application of high dimensional statistics and random matrix theory to problems in statistical genetics. Prior to joining Harvard, I completed my B.Stat (Hons.) and M.Stat from Indian Statistical Institute, Kolkata.

Currently, I am also working as a research assistant at the Centre for Value-based Health Care and Sciences at Mass General Brigham, working on projects related to emulated trial designs on the Medicare dataset. This research assistantship is co-advised by Sebastien Haneuse and Lidia Moura.

In my spare time, I enjoy solving and designing puzzles, playing badminton and table tennis, participating and conducting quizzes, and designing origami.
